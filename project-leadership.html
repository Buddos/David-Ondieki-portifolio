Chairperson, Faculty of Science

Complete Portfolio Description: Chairperson, Faculty of Science

Role Overview As the elected Chairperson of the Faculty of Science at Egerton University, I served as the primary academic and administrative leader for a complex unit comprising six core departments: Biochemistry & Molecular Biology, Biological Sciences, Chemistry, Computer Science, Mathematics, and Physics. In this "first among equals" capacity, I acted as the critical liaison between the faculty body and the University Administration (Dean, Provost, and Council), translating institutional strategy into operational reality while safeguarding academic integrity and faculty welfare.

Strategic Leadership & Governance I chaired the Faculty Board and key committees, driving the development and implementation of strategic plans aligned with the university’s vision for agricultural and scientific innovation. My leadership ensured orderly, transparent decision-making and effective policy execution. I fostered a culture of shared governance, resolving conflicts and building consensus among diverse academic specialties to unify the faculty around common goals for research excellence and teaching quality.

Academic Oversight & Curriculum Development I provided rigorous oversight for the faculty’s 17 undergraduate and 15 graduate programs, ensuring curricula remained relevant, accredited, and responsive to industry needs. I led collaborative efforts with department heads to revise educational programs, integrate modern pedagogical technologies, and enhance disciplinary literacy. This included coordinating assessment initiatives, promoting critical thinking, and ensuring that service courses delivered to other faculties met the highest scientific standards.

Resource Management & Operational Efficiency Managing the faculty’s human and financial capital, I oversaw budget preparation, monitored expenditures, and secured funding for research infrastructure and laboratory upgrades. I led recruitment and retention initiatives for high-caliber staff, supervised senior administrative personnel, and optimized resource allocation to support both teaching and research missions. My tenure was marked by improved fiscal transparency and the strategic alignment of resources with priority academic areas.

Stakeholder Engagement & External Representation I served as the chief ambassador for the Faculty of Science, representing its interests to the University Administration, alumni, industry partners, and the broader community. I actively cultivated external collaborations to secure internships, research grants, and community outreach opportunities. By maintaining open lines of communication with all stakeholders, I successfully advocated for the faculty’s needs and promoted its achievements, enhancing the unit’s reputation and impact within the region and beyond.
