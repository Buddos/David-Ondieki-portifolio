Nanoparticle Extraction Using Kale

2025
Egerton University
Supervisor: Dr. Caroline Kosgei

Project Overview

This undergraduate research project focused on extracting nanoparticles using kale leaves from silver nitrate solutions. The project encompassed comprehensive compound identification, efficiency testing, procedure documentation, and detailed result analysis using Microsoft Excel. The work was conducted under the supervision of Dr. Caroline Kosgei at Egerton University.

Methodology

Extraction Process

Kale leaves were selected as the biological medium for nanoparticle extraction due to their natural reduction properties. The leaves were processed and introduced to silver nitrate solutions to facilitate nanoparticle formation through biological reduction mechanisms.

The methodology involved multiple stages:

  • Sample preparation and standardization of kale leaf material
  • Solution preparation with controlled silver nitrate concentrations
  • Reaction monitoring and timing optimization
  • Nanoparticle characterization and isolation
  • Efficiency measurement through spectroscopic analysis
  • Data compilation and statistical analysis
